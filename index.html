Background

From implementation to insight — then into product.

Most APMs come in having studied product. I came in having built it — deploying AI workflows at the NFL, designing chatbot architectures at Evercore, and briefing the NFL Commissioner and C-suite on platform strategy.

That practitioner's lens shapes how I approach product work. I've sat in every seat at the table — the engineer troubleshooting an integration, the trainer driving adoption, the analyst building the dashboard that changes leadership's mind. That's a distinct advantage when it comes to writing requirements, validating features, and knowing where solutions break down before they ship.

I'm now moving deliberately into product management, with a focus on data and AI solutions — environments where the technical complexity is high and the business stakes are even higher.

How I Work

A practitioner's approach to product.

1
Discover
Deep stakeholder interviews. Workflow shadowing. Finding the gap between what people say they need and what the system actually does.
2
Define
Translating discovery into precise requirements — functional, technical, and governance. Prioritized by impact and feasibility, not intuition.
3
Build
Hands-on collaboration with engineering. I can read a business rule, trace an API call, and ask the right questions when something's off.
4
Validate
UAT that actually tests the use case, not just the spec. Data quality checks. Edge cases that only practitioners think to try.
5
Adopt
A shipped feature no one uses is a failed product. Training, change management, and executive buy-in are part of the deliverable.
